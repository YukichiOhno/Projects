Old Fashioned

Even if you know nothing about cocktails, chances are you've at least heard of an Old Fashioned. Made with just four ingredients - whiskey, sugar, Angostura bitters, and an optional (but recommended) orange peel or Luxardo cherry garnish.

Ingredients

  • 2 ounces bourbon or rye whiskey
  • 1/4 ounce simple syrup
  • 2 dashes Angostura bitters
  • Orange peel or Luxardo cherry, for garnish (optional)

Instructions

  1. Add simple syrup and bitters to a rocks glass. Fill the glass with ice and stir to combine.
  2. Add the bourbon or rye and stir for about 30 seconds to chill and dilute. Garnish with orange peel or Luxardo cherry, if desired.
  3. Garnish with orange peel or Luxardo cherry, if desired and enjoy!.

Amaretto Sour

In its most basic form, it features a sweet component, the amaretto, and a sour component, usually lemon juice. You can make it slightly sweeter with simple syrup, stronger with a harder liquor, like whiskey, or give it a silky texture and smooth mouthfeel with an egg white.

Ingredients

  • 2 ounces amaretto
  • 1 ounce fresh lemon juice
  • 1 egg white (optional)
  • Ice, for the shaker and for serving
  • Lemon wheel, for garnish
  • Brandied cherry, for garnish

Instructions

  1. Combine the amaretto, lemon juice, and egg white in a cocktail shaker without ice.
  2. Shake vigorously until frothy, 20 to 30 seconds.
  3. Then, gently add ice until it fills the cocktail shaker by half.
  4. Shake again to chill the cocktail, at least 20 seconds.
  5. Strain the cocktail into a rocks glass filled with ice, giving the shaker a few hard shakes to get as much foam out as possible.
  6. Garnish with a lemon wheel and brandied cherry.

Ranch Water

This is quite possibly the most refreshing drink I've ever had, and it has a little buzz to it without being overly alcoholic. A simple mix of tequila, mineral water, and lime is all it takes to whip up this summer sipper.

Ingredients

  • 3 ounces Blanco tequila, divided
  • 1 lime, quartered
  • 8 ounces Topo Chico mineral water
  • Lime wheels

Instructions

  1. Fill two highball or tumbler glasses with ice. Add 1 1/2 ounces of tequila to each cocktail glass.
  2. Squeeze one lime segment into each glass. You will have half a lime leftover, save it for your second round, use it as garnish or if you prefer more lime, add more!
  3. Top the glasses off with Topo Chico mineral water. Add a few lime wedges or wheels to each drink. Serve immediately.
